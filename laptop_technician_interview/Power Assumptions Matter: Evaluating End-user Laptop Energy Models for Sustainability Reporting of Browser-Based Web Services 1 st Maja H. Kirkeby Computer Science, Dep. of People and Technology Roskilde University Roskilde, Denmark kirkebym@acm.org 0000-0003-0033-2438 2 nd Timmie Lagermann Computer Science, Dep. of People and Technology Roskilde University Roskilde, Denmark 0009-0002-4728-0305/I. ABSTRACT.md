I. INTRODUCTION

Digital sustainability reporting has become central to the

evaluation of web-based services. Governments and organi-

zations increasingly depend on simplified models to estimate

energy use and greenhouse gas emissions. Two such models

are the Danish Agency of Digital Government’s Digst frame-

work [1] and the UK-based DIMPACT framework [2], [3]

based on [4]. However, despite their growing role, the empir-

ical validation of these models is limited. Without systematic

assessment, it remains unclear how accurately they capture the

energy usage of real-world web interactions.
